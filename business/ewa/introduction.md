# Early Wage Access (EWA)

Give your employees access to earned wages before payday, improving financial wellness and employee satisfaction.

## What is EWA?

Early Wage Access allows employees to withdraw a portion of their earned wages before the scheduled payday. This helps employees:
- Handle unexpected expenses
- Avoid predatory payday loans
- Improve financial wellness
- Reduce financial stress

## How It Works

### For Employers
1. **Enable EWA** - Toggle EWA in your payroll settings
2. **Set Limits** - Define maximum withdrawal percentages (typically 50-80%)
3. **Fund Escrow** - Deposit payroll funds to the escrow account
4. **Automatic Reconciliation** - Deductions are automated on payday

### For Employees
1. **Request Withdrawal** - Access via the employee portal or mobile app
2. **Instant Approval** - Eligible amounts are calculated automatically
3. **Receive Funds** - Money arrives instantly via Solana or bank transfer
4. **Automatic Deduction** - Amount is deducted from next paycheck

## Eligibility Criteria

Employees become eligible based on:
- Days worked since last payroll
- Current earned wage amount
- No pending overdrafts
- Employment status (full-time/part-time)

## Setting Up EWA

### Step 1: Enable in Settings
Navigate to **Payroll > EWA Settings** and enable the feature.

### Step 2: Configure Limits
Set the maximum percentage of earned wages employees can withdraw (recommended: 50-70%).

### Step 3: Fund Escrow Account
Transfer payroll funds to the Corridor escrow account. This ensures instant withdrawals.

### Step 4: Employee Onboarding
Employees will see the EWA option in their portal after their first payroll cycle.
